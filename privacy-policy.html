Types of Data Collected

Depending on how a user interacts with the website, IBCCS may collect personal data such as first name, last name, email address, phone number, profession, residency country, postal code, city, address, company name, website, field of activity, username, and similar contact or identification details.

The website may also collect usage data automatically, including technical information about the browser, device, IP address, time spent on pages, navigation paths, and related interaction data.

Some information may be mandatory for IBCCS to provide its services. Where data is optional, users may choose not to provide it without affecting general website availability.

The website may use cookies or similar tracking tools to deliver requested services, support functionality, and improve user experience. Users who provide third-party data confirm that they have the right to do so.

How Data Is Processed

IBCCS applies appropriate technical and organizational security measures to protect personal data from unauthorized access, disclosure, alteration, or destruction.

Data processing may be carried out using computers, IT systems, and service providers that support the operation of the website, including administration, sales, marketing, legal, system administration, hosting, communications, and technical support functions.

Legal Bases for Processing

  • Where the user has given consent for one or more specific purposes.
  • Where processing is necessary to perform a contract or pre-contractual measures.
  • Where processing is required to comply with a legal obligation.
  • Where processing is necessary for legitimate interests pursued by IBCCS or a third party.

Place of Processing and Transfers

Data may be processed at IBCCS operating offices or wherever the parties involved in processing are located. This may include transfers to countries other than the user’s own country. Users may contact IBCCS to learn more about applicable safeguards and legal bases for such transfers.

Retention

Personal data is stored only for as long as needed to fulfill the purpose for which it was collected, including legal, contractual, and legitimate-interest obligations. Once retention periods expire, the relevant data will be deleted or otherwise removed.

Purposes of Processing

Personal data may be processed in order to:

  • Provide IBCCS services and respond to user requests.
  • Contact users about enquiries, quotes, or other requests submitted through the website.
  • Manage contacts, mailing lists, newsletters, and other communications.
  • Support the operation, maintenance, and security of the website.

Contact Forms, Mailing Lists, and Newsletters

When users complete a contact form or sign up for communications, their submitted data may be used to respond to enquiries and send information related to IBCCS services, updates, or promotional materials.

User Rights

Users may have the right to:

  • Withdraw consent at any time where processing is based on consent.
  • Object to processing where a lawful basis other than consent is used.
  • Access the personal data being processed and obtain a copy.
  • Request rectification or updating of inaccurate data.
  • Request restriction of processing in certain circumstances.
  • Request deletion of personal data where applicable.
  • Receive personal data in a structured, commonly used format and request transfer where technically feasible.
  • Lodge a complaint with the competent data protection authority.

Requests to exercise these rights may be sent to IBCCS using the contact details above. Such requests are generally handled free of charge and within a reasonable timeframe.

Additional Information

Legal Action

Personal data may be used by IBCCS for legal purposes, including in court or in the stages leading to possible legal action arising from improper use of the website or its services.

System Logs and Maintenance

For operation and maintenance purposes, the website and related third-party services may collect system logs and similar technical records, including IP addresses and usage details.

Do Not Track

This website does not currently support “Do Not Track” requests. Users should review the privacy policies of any third-party services used through the website to understand how those services handle such signals.

Changes to This Policy

IBCCS may update this privacy policy from time to time. Any updates may be published on this page and, where appropriate, communicated through the website or by using available contact information.
