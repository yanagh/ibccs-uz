## Problems & Pain Points
**Core problem:**
Clients want to operate in Uzbekistan, but local tax, legal, and regulatory frameworks can be difficult to interpret and apply correctly in practice.

**Why alternatives fall short:**
- Generic advisors may not adapt recommendations to the client's actual business model.
- DIY setup can create tax, legal, licensing, or structural mistakes early on.
- International businesses often need both local execution and cross-border context, which many providers do not bridge well.

**What it costs them:**
Lost time, weak structuring decisions, compliance risk, unnecessary administrative friction, and higher costs later when mistakes need to be corrected.

**Emotional tension:**
Uncertainty, fear of making the wrong early decisions, and pressure to move quickly without compromising compliance.

## Competitive Landscape
**Direct:**
Local tax, legal, accounting, and registration firms in Uzbekistan.

**Secondary:**
Freelance accountants, law offices handling narrow scopes, and informal setup support providers.

**Indirect:**
Internal self-management by founders or finance teams, or remote advisors without local execution capability.

**How competitors may fall short:**
Some providers are too narrow, too generic, or too disconnected from the client's cross-border and long-term business needs.
